# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Serious strength athletes and gym-goers who track every set, want to understand what their data means, and care about community with people who train the same way. They are in the gym with the app open during a workout (mid-set, hands possibly chalked), or reviewing their training later on the couch. The primary task on any screen is either "log this set fast" or "understand what this data is telling me."

## Product Purpose

A strength training platform that closes the loop between tracking, analysis, and community. Users log workouts with minimal friction, explore what their training variables actually correlate with (rest time, RIR, exercise selection), and share findings and sessions with a community that trains the same way. Success looks like: a user finishes a workout, sees something interesting about their data, posts it to the community, and gets a useful response.

## Brand Personality

Focused, precise, and earned — not aspirational fitness marketing. The app speaks like a knowledgeable training partner, not a coach selling a program. Personality in three words: Serious · Precise · Communal.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS: blue buttons, Inter at 400 weight, cream/beige backgrounds, hero-metric dashboards
- MyFitnessPal: cluttered, ad-heavy, everything competes for attention
- Strava: consumer fitness brand playbook, orange-everything, leaderboard-as-product
- Bodybuilding.com: aggressive gym-bro aesthetic, loud, overwrought

## Design Principles

1. **UI disappears into the task.** During a workout, the interface should offer near-zero resistance. Every extra tap is a friction cost paid with cold muscles.
2. **Data earns decoration.** Visuals (color, emphasis, charts) appear because something is meaningful, not as default decoration.
3. **Precision without coldness.** Monospace numbers, tight grids, and density are appropriate — but the app should feel like it was made by someone who trains, not by someone who builds dashboards.
4. **Community is a first-class surface.** The feed isn't an afterthought or a social obligation. Content shared here (workouts, PRs, studies) is legitimately useful information.
5. **Palette before patterns.** Color decisions are made at a system level, not component-by-component. Each major surface has a committed palette identity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Body text must meet 4.5:1 contrast. All interactive elements have visible focus states. Rest timer and completion animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. No color as the sole signal for state (done/active/error use shape + color together).
